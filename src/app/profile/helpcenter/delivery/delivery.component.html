What is Incoterm

International commercial terms, or Incoterms, are published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) in order to ease international trade. Incoterms, which are widely accepted, eliminate misunderstandings in international commerce contracts by outlining the responsibilities of both buyers and sellers.

What is DDP delivery

When goods are delivered "delivered duty paid" (DDP), the seller bears all liability, risk, and expense up until the buyer picks them up or transfers ownership at the destination port.Â Expenses incurred when delivery to a predetermined location in the buyer's nation, including shipping charges, export and import tariffs, insurance, and any other costs, are covered by this agreement. It is an international transportation standard contract, or incoterm.

What is Ex-Work Shipping

Ex Works (EXW) is a phrase used in international trade to describe the situation where a seller makes a product available at a specified place, but the buyer is responsible for the costs associated with transportation. One of the 11 current Incoterms (International Commercial Terms), published by the International Chamber of Commerce, is "Ex Works" (EXW).
