    • An RFQ is a formal request made by a buyer to potential suppliers for quotations or bids for specific products or services. It's a standard procurement process to gather pricing and terms from suppliers.
    • RFQs help buyers compare quotes from multiple suppliers, ensuring they get the best value for their purchase. It also provides a structured way to communicate requirements.
    • Typically, you create an RFQ document outlining your requirements, including specifications, quantities, delivery terms, and any other relevant details. Then, you send this document to selected suppliers.
    • A well-prepared RFQ includes clear product or service descriptions, quantity, quality standards, delivery timelines, and any specific terms or conditions.
    • No, an RFQ is usually not legally binding. It's a solicitation for offers, and it becomes binding when you accept a supplier's quotation and create a purchase order.
    • Evaluate responses based on factors like price, quality, delivery times, supplier reputation, and terms. Choose the supplier that best meets your needs.
    • While an RFQ focuses on pricing and specific product details, an RFP is a more comprehensive request that includes details about the project or service and seeks a solution or proposal from suppliers.
