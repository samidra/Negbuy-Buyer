    • RFQs help buyers compare quotes from multiple suppliers, ensuring they get the best value for their purchase. It also provides a structured way to communicate requirements.
    • Typically, you create an RFQ document outlining your requirements, including specifications, quantities, delivery terms, and any other relevant details. Then, you send this document to selected suppliers.
    • A well-prepared RFQ includes clear product or service descriptions, quantity, quality standards, delivery timelines, and any specific terms or conditions.
